Global CBDC Readiness Index (GCRI)

Methodology – Version 1.0

1. Objective

The Global CBDC Readiness Index (GCRI) is designed to measure the relative readiness, maturity, and adoption of central bank digital currencies (CBDCs) across selected economies.

The index provides a structured, transparent, and repeatable framework to assess CBDC development beyond individual announcements or pilot headlines.

The GCRI is a reference benchmark only and does not represent a financial instrument, investment product, or tradable asset.

2. Index Universe

The index universe consists of countries whose central banks meet at least one of the following conditions:

  • A retail or wholesale CBDC has been launched
  • An advanced pilot program is underway
  • A formally approved CBDC roadmap has been published

The initial universe is intentionally limited to ensure data quality and consistency. Expansion of the universe may occur in accordance with the governance rules outlined below.

3. Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

Inclusion Criteria

A country is eligible for inclusion if reliable public information confirms:

  • Live CBDC issuance
  • Government- or central bank-approved pilot deployment
  • An official CBDC policy or implementation framework

Exclusion Criteria

The index explicitly excludes:

  • Cryptocurrencies
  • Private stablecoins
  • Unofficial or speculative digital currency proposals
  • Tokens not issued or backed by a central bank

4. Index Factors and Weighting

Each country is evaluated across five core factors. Scores are assigned on a standardized scale and combined using fixed weights.

Factor Description Weight
CBDC Status Stage of development (live, pilot, design) 30%
Usage & Transaction Scale Observed or reported usage levels 25%
Legal & Regulatory Framework Statutory and regulatory preparedness 20%
Cross-Border & Wholesale Integration Interbank and international settlement initiatives 15%
Technical Infrastructure Readiness Platform maturity and operational capability 10%
Total 100%

5. Scoring Methodology

Each factor is scored on a 0–100 scale based on predefined, transparent criteria.

  • 90–100: Fully live and operational at scale
  • 60–89: Advanced pilot or limited production use
  • 30–59: Early-stage pilot or controlled testing
  • 0–29: Research or conceptual phase

Scores are derived solely from verifiable public disclosures and do not rely on projections or forecasts.

6. Index Calculation

Each country’s aggregate score is calculated as the weighted sum of its factor scores.

Index Value = (Current Aggregate Score ÷ Base Aggregate Score) × Base Index Level

Base Index Level: 1,000
Base Date: Index launch date

7. Rebalancing and Review

  • The index is reviewed and updated on a quarterly basis.
  • Rebalancing reflects changes in CBDC status, usage, or regulatory developments.
  • Extraordinary updates occur only in the event of a full CBDC launch or termination.

8. Data Sources

Primary sources include:

  • Central bank publications and official statements
  • Bank for International Settlements (BIS)
  • International Monetary Fund (IMF)
  • Government and regulatory documents

9. Governance and Methodology Changes

  • This methodology is versioned and publicly available
  • Material changes require advance disclosure
  • Historical index values are maintained for continuity
